The Music

The Story Begins

On October 20, 2015, TWICE debuted with the extended play The Story Begins, with the title track "Like Ooh-Ahh." Though they had a budding fandom, the EP was far from their most successful tracks.

Page Two and The Rise to Fame

On April 25, 2016, TWICE released their second EP, Page Two, with the title track "Cheer Up." This track received much more love and support than their debut, and lifted them into the spotlight. From this point on, TWICE was South Korea's "nation's girl group." Later, they'd begin to be referred to as "Asia's girl group."

"TT": The Root of Global Stardom

TWICECOASTER: Lane 1 was TWICE's third EP, released on October 24, 2016 with the title track "TT" touched the hearts of listeners and viewers worldwide, with the catchy lyrics and melody and a cute and simple choreography. This track was the root of TWICE becoming a global girl group. TWICECOASTER: Lane 2 was a repackage of the previous EP, released February 20, 2017, adding the title track "KNOCK KNOCK" and the b-side "Ice Cream."

"SIGNAL": Song of The Year

On May 15, 2017, TWICE cameback with SIGNAL, their fourth EP. The title track of the EP, "SIGNAL" received Song of The Year at 2017's Mnet Asian Music Awards, showing how successful the track was.

The First Japanese Release

On October 18, 2017, TWICE released their first Japanese single "One More Time." The song was very successful, as listeners kept wanting to play it "One More Time."

The First Studio Album

Twicetagram, released on October 30, 2017, was TWICE's first full studio album. They cameback with the title track "LIKEY," which was the first music video they filmed overseas. The song yet again had catchy lyrics and melody, and addictive dance moves which grabbed the attention of fans worldwide. On December 11 of the same year, they repackaged the album and added two new singles: "Heart Shaker" and "Merry & Happy," just in time for the holidays.

Candy Pop

Candy Pop was another Japanese single, which received love from fans worldwide not only due to the catchy and addictive nature of the song, but also the music video, which portrayed the members as anime characters.

What is Love? TWICE is Love

On April 9, 2018, TWICE came back with the EP What is Love?, with a title track with the same name. This song granted them their third Song of the Year win and was also well received. Later, on July 9 of the same year, TWICE repackaged the EP with the title track "Dance The Night Away." This was TWICE's first summer-themed release, and as a result was well received because of the new concept (and a catchy melody and addictive choreo).

BDZ

On September 12, 2018, TWICE released their first Japanese studio album, titled BDZ, and shortened form of "bulldozer." The album was well received, as per usual.

Yes or Yes

On November 5, 2018, TWICE released "Yes or Yes," and then later repackaged it as The Year of "YES" with the title "The Best Thing I Ever Did"for the holidays. The EP may not have been as memorable as others, but it was nontheless successful.

I Fancy You

On April 22, 2019, TWICE released the EP Fancy You, with title track "FANCY". This song took the industry by storm. Everywhere you looked, there was someone dancing to "FANCY", listening to it, or watching the music video.

TWICE is "Happy Happy" to "Breakthrough"

On July 17, 2019, TWICE released two Japanese singles simultaneously. The two singles had starkly contrasting concepts, "Happy Happy" being bright and summery, and "Breakthrough" being dark and edgy. TWICE had never done a dark concept like "Breakthrough" before, and as a result it was extremely successful. It was so successful that they released a Korean version of the song to perfom in Korea.

Feel Special

On September 23, 2019, TWICE cameback with Feel Speical, with a title track of the same name. This EP was the first EP in which TWICE was incomplete, as member Mina went on hiatus to focus on her mental health. Despite only promoting as eight members, the track was still successful.

&Twice

On October 18, 2019, TWICE released their second Japanese studio album, &Twice, with the title track "Fake & True." The song was successful, and many netizens made memes from screencaps of the music video.

MORE & MORE of TWICE

On June 1, 2020, TWICE released the EP MORE & MORE, with a title track of the same name. The EP was more experimental and edgy than TWICE's usual style, but still had elements of TWICE's signature cute concept.

The Second Studio Album

TWICE's second full length album titled Eyes Wide Open, showcases a more mature and versatile TWICE, with lyrics written by the members themselves and the title track "I Can't Stop Me." Stuyvesant's own Stuy Squad did a cover of the title during this year's Stuy Squad Showcase.

The Latest (as of February 2021)

TWICE's latest release as of February 2021 is their single "CRY FOR ME." This single is different from the rest of their songs as they debuted at the 2020 Mnet Asian Music Awards. Debuting a song at an awards show is not a common thing in the K-Pop industry, which in turn generated much buzz for TWICE. If TWICE's performance garnered enough views on YouTube, they would release the song officially, which happened quite quickly.
